PULSEGUARD

Early warning for the operating room · research prototype
Aastik Rajan · Johns Hopkins University · aastikc15@gmail.com · live demo + code: aastikrajan.github.io/pulseguard

What it is. Software that reads the live vital signs during surgery and warns about 4 minutes before the patient gets into trouble — and says which problem (out of 9) and why. A research prototype, not a medical device.

Why it matters. Today's monitors only alarm after a number crosses a line, and they alarm so often that staff stop trusting them. The one product that predicts ahead (Edwards HPI, in 10,000+ hospitals) predicts only one problem, needs an arterial line, and after 8 years still has no trial proving it helps patients.

What I built — alone, on public data (VitalDB, 5,151 surgeries)

Warn before any deteriorationAUPRC 0.454 · AUROC 0.849 · ~4.3 min early
Get the alarms right62–71% real · vs ~12% in the field
Say which of 9 events is comingAUROC 0.74–0.96
Work at another hospital, no retrainingAUROC 0.78–0.80 (Seoul→UC-Irvine)
Does low blood pressure cause kidney injury?mostly no · RR 1.77 → 1.07

What's different. No existing system does all of this together — many events, works across hospitals, honest cause analysis, and trustworthy alarms. And I held it to a strict honesty bar: I locked the test set, threw out a model that was cheating, and reported every failure — including that hypotension is basically unpredictable (shown 7 independent ways). The honesty is the point.

One practical upshot. The two signals that matter most are heart rate and SpO₂ (pulse-ox) — both cheap. So a low-cost version (ECG + pulse-ox only) could work where arterial lines aren't available.
